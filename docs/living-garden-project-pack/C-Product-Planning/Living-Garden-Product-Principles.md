# Living Garden Product Principles v1.0

## Core Experience

Living Garden begins as a mostly empty piece of land.

As the customer progresses, the same scene gradually becomes richer.

The customer should enjoy noticing:

- a new sprout
- more grass
- a flower
- a mushroom
- an insect
- a small animal
- a garden object

The product should not over-explain these changes.

## Primary Principle

The joy comes from noticing, not from being notified.

## Simplicity Rules

Do not add:

- complex storylines
- long dialogue
- daily chapter text
- achievement popups
- excessive celebration animations
- complicated game mechanics
- multiple new focal elements in one day

## Daily Change Rule

Each day should introduce one clearly noticeable primary change.

Some days may also include small supporting adjustments, but only one new element should act as the day's visual focus.

## Accumulation Rule

Day 20 must include everything unlocked from Day 1 through Day 20.

The garden never resets within the chapter.

## Fixed Scene Rule

Use the same primary garden scene and camera composition.

Do not change the entire camera angle every day.

Customers should be able to compare today's scene with yesterday's scene easily.

## Visual Richness Rule

The garden should become progressively richer, but not chaotic.

Even on Day 35:

- preserve visual breathing space
- maintain a clear visual hierarchy
- avoid placing objects everywhere
- keep greenery as the dominant visual foundation

## Customer-Facing Text

Keep customer-facing text minimal.

Allowed examples:

- Chapter 1
- progress such as 18 / 35

Avoid explaining every unlocked object.
