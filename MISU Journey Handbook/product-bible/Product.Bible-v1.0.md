We are not building another weight loss application.



We are building a health companion that supports customers every day.



\---

To become the most trusted digital health companion for every XMEGAMI customer.



Our goal is to create a journey that customers enjoy following, instead of forcing themselves to complete.



\---



\# Product Philosophy



Weight loss is not the final goal.



Healthy habits are.



Every feature inside MISU Journey should encourage consistency instead of perfection.



The system should reduce stress instead of creating pressure.



The customer should always feel encouraged, never judged.



\---

\## Simplicity



Keep every feature simple.



If customers need to think too much, the design is too complicated.



\---



\## Consistency



Small actions repeated every day are more valuable than perfect actions done occasionally.



\---



\## Encouragement



The system should celebrate progress.



Never punish customers for missing a day.



\---



\## Education



Teach customers why.



Not just tell customers what to do.



\---



\## Sustainability



Every recommendation should be something customers can continue for years.



Never encourage unhealthy or extreme behaviour.

\# Chapter 2 | Journey Framework



\## Purpose



MISU Journey is designed as a long-term health journey platform, not a single weight loss challenge.



The framework must be flexible enough to support future products, programmes and customer goals without requiring major system redesign.



\---



\## Journey Philosophy



A Journey represents a meaningful period where a customer works towards a specific health goal with guidance from the XMEGAMI ecosystem.



A Journey is not limited to weight loss.



Different Journey Plans may exist in the future to support different customer needs.



\---



\## Journey Plan



A Journey Plan defines the structure of a customer's current programme.



Each Journey Plan includes:



\- Journey Name

\- Journey Category

\- Duration

\- Objectives

\- Required Tasks

\- Completion Rules



Phase 1 includes:



\- 60 Days Journey

\- 90 Days Journey



Future Journey Plans may include:



\- Healthy Lifestyle Journey

\- Weight Management Journey

\- Gut Health Journey

\- Women's Wellness Journey

\- Hair Recovery Journey

\- Future MISU programmes



The framework must allow new Journey Plans to be added without changing the overall system architecture.



\---



\## Journey Lifecycle



Every customer follows the same lifecycle.



Registration



↓



Choose Journey Plan



↓



Complete Journey



↓



Journey Completion Review



↓



Customer chooses:



• Start another Journey Plan



or



• Enter Lifestyle Mode



\---



\## Continuous Journey



A customer's history never resets.



The following records continue across multiple Journey Plans:



\- Body Progress

\- Weight History

\- Check-in History

\- Learning Progress

\- Achievement History



Only the active Journey Plan changes.



This ensures every customer has one continuous health timeline instead of multiple disconnected records.



\---



\## Lifestyle Mode



Lifestyle Mode is optional.



It is never entered automatically.



After completing a Journey Plan, customers decide whether they wish to:



\- Continue improving through another Journey Plan



or



\- Enter Lifestyle Mode to maintain their results.



Lifestyle Mode is designed for long-term healthy living rather than active transformation.



\---



\## Framework Principle



The system is designed to support customers throughout their entire health journey.



Journeys may change.



Goals may change.



Products may change.



The customer's history should always remain connected.



\---



\## Journey Consistency



Every Journey is built on consistency rather than perfection.



The system should encourage customers to consistently participate in their Journey instead of measuring whether they perfectly follow a predefined product dosage.



Different Coaches may recommend different product usage strategies based on each customer's individual situation.



Therefore, the system should evaluate Journey Consistency rather than assuming one fixed product consumption schedule.



Journey Consistency represents how actively a customer continues participating throughout their Journey.



Examples include:



• Morning Check-ins

• Evening Reflections

• Product Usage

• Body Progress

• Other Journey Activities



The purpose is to identify customers who may gradually lose momentum so Coaches can provide timely encouragement.



Journey Consistency is different from Repurchase Reminder.



Repurchase Reminder focuses on inventory.



Journey Consistency focuses on participation.



\# Chapter 3 | Decision Framework



\## Purpose



This framework defines how product decisions should be made throughout the development of MISU Journey.



Every new feature, improvement or product idea should be evaluated against these principles before entering development.



The objective is to maintain a simple, scalable and customer-first product.



\---



\## Principle 1



Customer First



Every decision begins with one question:



"Does this improve the customer's experience?"



Technology exists to support customers, not create additional burden.



\---



\## Principle 2



Guide, Don't Force



Whenever possible, guide customers instead of forcing behaviour.



The system should encourage good habits through thoughtful design rather than strict restrictions.



\---



\## Principle 3



Reduce Friction



Every new feature should reduce unnecessary actions.



If a feature adds extra steps without creating meaningful value, it should be reconsidered.



The best experience is usually the simplest one.



\---



\## Principle 4



Build for Tomorrow



Solve today's problem while leaving room for future expansion.



Avoid over-engineering.



Avoid designing only for today's requirements.



\---



\## Principle 5



One Module, One Responsibility



Each module should have one clear responsibility.



If a feature becomes a separate business capability, it should become its own module.



This keeps the system maintainable as it grows.



\---



\## Principle 6



Technology Should Amplify People



Technology should make Coaches more effective, not replace them.



The system should automate repetitive work so Coaches can spend more time providing genuine support and care.



\---



\## Principle 7



Professional Builds Trust.

Warmth Builds Companionship.



Every interaction should combine professional guidance with genuine encouragement.



Customers should learn something useful while feeling supported throughout their Journey.



\---



\## Before Every Product Decision



Before approving any new feature, ask:



\- Does this make healthy living easier?

\- Does this reduce Coach workload?

\- Does this improve long-term consistency?

\- Can a new customer understand it within seconds?

\- Will this still make sense three years from now?



If the answer is "No" to most of these questions, the feature should be redesigned before development.
